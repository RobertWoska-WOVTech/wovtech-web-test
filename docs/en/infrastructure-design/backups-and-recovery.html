Backups and recovery: what companies think they have vs. what they can really restore

A backup is not security. Without recovery it is just a stored file. In infrastructure design, the key question is what can be restored and how fast.

A working backup is measured by recovery capability, not by a green job status.

Questions worth answering

  • What exactly are we backing up (data, configuration, infra state, secrets)?
  • What can we restore automatically and what still requires manual improvisation?
  • What are the RPO/RTO values for each service?
  • When did we last test recovery and what was the result?
  • Where are recovery credentials and keys stored?

Typical risks in practice

  • Backup without recovery: backups exist, but restore was never tested.
  • Shared access: the backup platform uses the same accounts as production.
  • Identity not protected: IAM, AD or cloud accounts are missing from recovery scope.
  • No runbook: recovery becomes improvisation during incidents.
