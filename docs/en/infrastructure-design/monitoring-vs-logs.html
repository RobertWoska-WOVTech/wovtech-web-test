Monitoring vs logs: what they solve and why one fails without the other

Monitoring and logs are often mixed together. In infrastructure design they answer different questions: monitoring = what is happening, logs = why it is happening.

Monitoring tells you there is a problem and what the impact is. Logs tell you why the problem happened.

Questions worth answering

  • What is a critical service for us and what is merely nice-to-have?
  • Who responds to alerts and what reaction time is expected?
  • What is an incident and what is just information?
  • Do we have a runbook or at least a minimum triage path?
  • Can we use logs to prove the cause quickly, not only see the symptom?

Practical conclusion

  • The most common mistake is having a lot of data with no context, which creates noise and panic.
  • A good design defines a minimum set of metrics, alerts and logs that are actionable.
  • If you are starting out, it is better to have a small number of alerts you really act on than hundreds you ignore.
