NTP: why time is critical in infrastructure

Time synchronisation is a tier-0 capability: it directly affects security, auditability and the ability to troubleshoot incidents quickly.

Incorrect time breaks authentication, certificates and log correlation – and then incidents are investigated blind.

Questions worth answering

  • What is the primary time source and what is the backup if it fails?
  • Which systems are time-sensitive (SSO/auth, certificates, logs, databases)?
  • How do you monitor synchronisation (offset/drift), not just NTP reachability?
  • How is NTP handled in hybrid environments and where are trust boundaries?
  • Do you have a documented procedure for NTP incidents?

Typical impact of incorrect time

  • Longer triage: logs do not line up and event correlation is unreliable.
  • Security failures: issues with tokens, Kerberos/LDAP and certificate validity.
  • Audit and forensics: timelines become untrustworthy.
